Why are we interested in height and the overall survival period of the colorectal cancer patients?
Both environmental and genetic factors are implicated in the pathogenesis and prognosis of colorectal cancer. Since height is also influenced by these factors, we believe that it may be of some interest to determine whether correlation exists between heights and overall survival. We used the open-access resource cBioPortal to obtain clinical data of colorectal cancer patients (n=632). Unfortunately, height information was not recorded in all patients. We hypothesize that no significant correlation will be identified.

The cBioPortal dataset

cBioPortal page: http://www.cbioportal.org/study.do?cancer_study_id=coadread_tcga#summary
Correlation of Heights and Overall Survival of Colorectal Cancer

Result: No apparent correlation between heights and overall survival months of colorectal cancer observed as expected. While patients taller than 180 cm did not survive past 100 months, it may be due to the small sample size (n=11). Further statistical analysis will not be necessary at this time.
